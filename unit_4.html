1. Introduction to Genetic Algorithms (GA)

Definition: Genetic Algorithms are adaptive search algorithms that belong to the larger family of Evolutionary Algorithms. They are based on the evolutionary ideas of natural selection and genetics.
ðŸŽ¯ Goal: It provides an approach to computing that parallels the remarkable ability of the human mind to reason and learn in an environment of uncertainty and imprecision.

2. Foundation of GA (Biological Analogy)

The concept of GA is based on an analogy with the genetic structure and behavior of chromosomes in a population.

ðŸ”‘ Key Principles:

1. Competition: Individuals in a population compete for resources and a mate.
2. Selection: Those individuals who are successful (the fittest) mate to create more offspring.
3. Propagation: Genes of the parents propagate throughout the generation. Sometimes parents create offspring that are better than either parent. Thus, each successive generation is more suited for their environment.

3. The Genetic Algorithm Process

ðŸ“‹ Algorithm Steps:

1

Initialization

Generate an initial population of possible solutions randomly.

2

Evaluation

Compute the fitness of each solution using a fitness function.

3

Selection

Choose the best individual solutions based on fitness to reproduce.

4

Crossover

Combine parts of 2 parent solutions to form new offspring.

5

Mutation

Introduce small random changes to maintain diversity in the population.

6

Replacement

Form a new population with the offspring and repeat the process.

4. Operators of Genetic Algorithm

A. Selection

Idea: To give preference to individuals with good fitness scores, allowing them to pass their genes to successive generations.

B. Crossover Operator

Idea: Represents "mating" between individuals. Two individuals are selected using the selection operator and combined randomly.

C. Mutation Operator

Key Idea: To insert random genes in offspring to maintain diversity in the population and avoid premature convergence.

5. Applications of GA in Machine Learning

GA is useful for non-linear, high dimensionality, and discontinuous spaces.

1
Feature Selection & Dimensionality Reduction: Choosing the best set of genes in bio-informatic datasets for disease prediction.
2
Hyperparameter Optimization: Optimizing architecture of Deep Learning (Network Layers, Neurons, Activation functions).
3
Neural Network Training: Evolving weights for Reinforcement Learning agents.
4
Rule-Based & Symbolic Learning: Used in Genetic Programming to evolve decision rules (e.g., Evolving if-then rules for classification).
5
Clustering: Finding optimal cluster centers in customer segmentation tasks.
6
Reinforcement Learning: Training agents in games or robotics without explicit gradient-based learning.
7
Model Structure Optimization: Searching for the best ML model architecture (e.g., Decision Trees, CNNs).
